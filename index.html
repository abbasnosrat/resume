Publications

TFNet: Few-Shot Identification of LTI Systems Based on Convolutional Neural Networks

Abbas Nosrat, , Ahmad Kalhor, Mohammad Reza Nayeri , Babak N Araabi • Under review at Journal of Process Control

Abstract

Recent research in system identification aims to reduce the amount of data required for identification and the persistence of exicitation while maintaining the identified modelâ€™s generalization and goodness of fit. This paper introduces TFNet as a few-shot identification method for industrial systems with two stages. TFNet enables both structural and parametric identification of inndustrial linear time-invariant systems from their pulse response. Furthermore, TFNet identifies properties such as existence of integrator and non-minimum-phase zeros in systems, as long as the order of the system is below two. Based on the comparison of TFNet to classical and rival methods using synthetic data, we show that TFNet outperforms the classical and rival methods by a wide margin. Furthermore, case studies on an air temperature control system and a two-tank control system laboratory setups demonstrate that TFNet estimates a close model to the ground truth despite colored measurement noise and without driving the system out of its operating point.
